how to use define "tab" delimiter with "cut" command in bash

Author: Arwin Lashawn Date: April 8, 2020  1:56:33 Category: tutorial

How this may be useful

Say you are working with a .tsv file and you only want the third field (or third column) only. You know how the “cut” command works but can’t seem to define “tab” for the “-d” flag in the terminal.

Important: One time I was working with some .tsv files but defining “tab” as the delimiter wouldn't work. This was because although the files have a .tsv extension, the separator used is actually “|”. For some context, I was working with BLAST results. A reminder to “less” the file first to get a sense of the delimiter used!

Two ways to do it

Examples below are for grabbing the third field of a .tsv file:

  1. In the terminal, Ctrl + V followed by Tab. Worked for me on Linux and Git Bash.

  2. Use $'\t'.

    You may be wondering. Are the dollar sign and single quotes actually necessary? Yes.

    Explanation: If you go ahead and just specify ‘\t’, you will get an error saying “the delimiter must be a single character”. By adding a dollar sign to it, you get a general form of $'string' which is treated uniquely. The result? The word expands to a string, and the backslash-escaped character, in this case “t”, is replaced by a “horizontal tab” according to the ANSI-C standard.
